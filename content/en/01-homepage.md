# Justice Access Foundation

**Expanding Access to Civil Justice in California**

---

## Mission Statement

Justice Access Foundation (JAF) is a California nonprofit public benefit corporation and IRS-recognized 501(c)(3) public charity. Our mission is to expand access to the civil legal system, protect due process rights, and work to prevent housing instability, wage theft, and exploitation affecting individuals and families in California who lack adequate legal resources.

---

## Program Areas

**Preventing Housing Instability**
JAF provides legal education, procedural information, and referral coordination to help low-income residents understand and assert their rights in housing-related proceedings, including tenancy disputes and unlawful detainer actions.

**Wage Protection and Economic Justice**
JAF supports worker access to legal information and resources for individuals facing unpaid wages, unlawful payroll deductions, or other forms of wage theft in violation of California and federal law.

**Procedural Fairness and Court Navigation Support**
JAF assists self-represented individuals in understanding civil court procedures, response deadlines, filing requirements, and their rights within the legal process. JAF does not provide legal representation.

**Public Legal Education and Literacy**
JAF produces and distributes plain-language legal information in English, Spanish, and Chinese (Simplified) to support informed participation in legal proceedings by California residents.

---

## Governance Integrity

Justice Access Foundation is governed by an independent volunteer Board of Directors. No director receives compensation. All governance decisions are made by the Board without influence from donors, commercial entities, or affiliated organizations. Financial activity is disclosed annually through IRS Form 990 public filings.
